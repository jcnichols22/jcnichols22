# Hi, I'm Josh.

IT admin, homelab tinkerer, and long-time believer that your data belongs to you, not a corporation.

I run a small self-hosted infrastructure from a server closet in my house—Proxmox nodes, Docker containers, Tailscale mesh VPN, the works. I spend most of my free time figuring out how to make technology cheaper, faster, and more private: whether that’s swapping paid cloud services for self-hosted alternatives, automating updates with Ansible, or just tweaking firewall rules at 11PM because I can't leave well enough alone.

The short version: I like systems that I control, tools that respect privacy, and building things that actually work. I also happen to do this stuff professionally as an IT admin, leaning toward security and networking.

This profile mixes both sides of that—the professional side and the “why would anyone pay for this when you can run it yourself” side.

---

## What I’m up to

- **Homelab** — A two-node Proxmox cluster (Diagon Alley) running containers for media, backups, DNS, ad-blocking, documentation, remote access, and more. All wired through Tailscale.
- **Automation** — Ansible playbooks for provisioning, patching, and maintaining services across the lab.
- **Security & privacy** — Wazuh, AdGuard, omada-controlled network segmentation, firewall rules, and a general obsession with knowing what my systems are doing.
- **Teaching & sharing** — I want to help people take control of their own data without needing a CS degree. If that sounds like your kind of content, stick around.
